Amazon Product Scrapping & Analysis

About the Project

This project, titled "Amazon Product Scraping & Analysis", focuses on extracting, cleaning, analyzing, and visualizing data from Amazon's laptop product listings. Using web scraping techniques with Selenium and BeautifulSoup, we gathered detailed information on various laptop models, including brand, screen size, RAM, HDD, operating system, and price. The data was cleaned and organized using Microsoft Excel, and insightful visualizations were created using Power BI.

The project aims to uncover trends and patterns in the laptop market, such as the distribution of screen sizes, the average price by brand and RAM, and the prevalence of graphic cards in laptops. Key findings include the popularity of specific screen sizes, the price differentiation based on RAM and operating system, and the dominance of certain brands in the market. This comprehensive analysis provides valuable insights into consumer preferences and market trends within the laptop segment.

1. Average Price Insights:

  1. Average Price by Brand:
    • Apple laptops are the most expensive, followed by Samsung and MSI.
    • Other brands like HP, Lenovo, Dell, and Honor have a more moderate average price.
  2. Average Price by RAM:
    • Laptops with 48 GB RAM have the highest average price, followed by those with 32 GB and 24 GB RAM.
    • 8 GB RAM models also tend to be on the higher side, while 4 GB models are the least expensive.
  3. Average Price by Operating System:
    • Windows 11 Pro has the highest average price among the operating systems.
    • DOS and JioOS are on the lower end.
  4. Average Price by Color:
    • Laptops in the Graphite color are the most expensive, followed by Blue and Grey.
    • Green and Black colored laptops have the lowest average prices.

2. Model Distribution:

  1. Count of Models by HDD:
    • 512 GB HDD models are the most common (71.05%), with 1 TB models following (17.52%).
    • There are fewer models with smaller HDD sizes.
  2. No. of Models by Brand:
    • The brand with the most models is Lenovo (23,6%), followed by HP(22.38%) and ASUS (18.25%).
    • Apple has fewer models listed (13.6%).
  3. No. of Models by Screen Size:
    • The 15.6-inch screen size is the most popular among all available options, with the highest number of models (175), likely due to its balance between portability and usability..
    • Smaller screen sizes such as 11.6, 12.3, and 12.5 inches have significantly fewer models available, suggesting that these sizes are less common or in lower demand.

3. Features:

  • Models with Graphic Card:
    • A significant majority (83.45%) of laptops do not come with a dedicated graphic card.
    • Only 16.3% include one, and a very small percentage (0.24%) have an advanced graphic card.

4. General Stats:

  • Brands: There are a total of 18 different brands available.
  • Number of Operating Systems: There are 7 different operating systems represented.

Conclusion:

  • High-end models are generally associated with higher RAM and the Apple brand.
  • Windows 11 Pro is the most expensive OS, suggesting it's positioned for premium laptops.
  • Most models come with standard HDD sizes and have basic graphic capabilities.
  • Grey and Silver seem to be the preferred colours, which might indicate a popular market preference for these colors.

Data Extraction Process

The data for this project was meticulously gathered using web scraping techniques. Below is an overview of the process:

  • Web Scraping with Selenium and BeautifulSoup:
    • Used Selenium to automate the browsing and extraction of HTML content from Amazon product pages (laptops).
    • Saved the HTML content of each page locally for further processing.
  • Data Parsing and Cleaning:
    • Employed BeautifulSoup to parse the HTML and extract specific details such as laptop brand, model, screen size, and price.
    • Compiled the extracted data into a structured format and saved it as a CSV file using Pandas.
  • Data Cleaning:
    • Used Microsoft Excel to clean and refine the dataset, ensuring the data was ready for analysis and visualization.
